Privacy statement

Important points:
  • Songs you save are shared publically using GitHub and GitHub Pages. (See GitHub's privacy statement)
  • Sheet music you enter into Hacklily is rendered on a server I run, and may be stored for debugging for up to 7 days. Aggregate information may be stored for longer for analytics and performance monitoring.
  • I collect your email address, IP address, GitHub username, and name to run the service, for analytics, and for debugging.
  • Separately, anonymous, cookieless usage counts (which features get used) are public at stats.hacklily.org — see "Analytics" below.

Privacy officer

I am Jocelyn Stericker. I operate the https://hacklily.org website ("Hacklily") and am the privacy officer. You can reach me at jocelyn@nettek.ca.

Information collection and use

While using Hacklily, you may log in using GitHub. If you do so, you will provide me with certain personally identifiable information that can be used to contact or identify you. Personally identifiable information may include, but is not limited to, your name, GitHub username, and email address ("Personal Information").

This Personal Information is used to run Hacklily, as well as for analytics and debugging. I will not contact you except as needed to run Hacklily effectively. I will not share your Personal Information with third parties except as needed to run Hacklily or use it to send you promotional messages.

Log data

LilyPond source entered in Hacklily is rendered using a copy of LilyPond running on a server. I may store a copy of songs source, the output, as well as your computer's Internet Protocol ("IP") address, browser type, browser type, browser version, and your Personal Information.

The songs themselves along with any associated Personal Information will be used for providing you with the service, as well as for debugging and may be stored for up to 7 days. Aggregate or anonymized information may be kept longer for analytics, so that (for example) I know how many monthly active users I have. I may share aggregate information with third parties.

DOM storage

I use DOM Storage (a technology similar to cookies) to store unsaved versions of your songs as well as Personal Information on your computer, even once you close Hacklily. DOM Storage must be enabled to log in via GitHub or store unsaved versions of songs. You can remove this information by logging out using the menu.

Analytics

Hacklily counts anonymous, aggregate usage with GoatCounter, a privacy-focused, open-source analytics tool. GoatCounter sets no cookies and stores no identifiers on your device, so there is nothing to consent to. It does not retain your IP address; it derives a salted, daily-rotating hash server-side to approximate unique visitors, then discards the address.

What is collected: which page you visit (the editor, the MusicXML importer, or the status page), and a small, fixed set of interaction events — for example that a render, a MusicXML import, a MIDI playback, or a GitHub save/publish happened, and whether the LilyPond version was "stable" or "unstable" — along with coarse technical details GoatCounter derives from the request (referring site, browser, screen size, and country). Song contents, filenames, GitHub usernames, and repository names are never included in this data, even though they often appear in Hacklily's own URLs.

The numbers are public, in the spirit of the project: stats.hacklily.org.

I do not act on Do Not Track or Global Privacy Control signals; see this explanation of why for the reasoning. Any content blocker that blocks GoatCounter will stop this counting.

Service Providers

This site is hosted using GitHub Pages. GitHub may store your IP address and other Personal Information when you access Hacklily.

By connecting Hacklily to GitHub, you agree to GitHub's privacy statement. Connecting Hacklily to GitHub allows you to save, store, and publish songs on GitHub and GitHub Pages.

Everything you save on Hacklily will be publically viewable in Hacklily and under your GitHub account. It will be publically stored on a GitHub repo associated with your GitHub account (and username) in the sheet-music repository associated with your account. All history will be stored. You can delete your history by deleting your sheet-music repository on GitHub.

Security

I try my best, but I cannot guarantee the absolute security of your Personal Information.

Links To Other Sites

If you click on a third party link, you will be directed to that third party's site. You might want to review the Privacy Statement of every site you visit.

Children's privacy

Hacklily does not address anyone under the age of 13 ("Children"). I do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and you are aware that your Children has provided Hacklily with Personal Information, please contact me. If I discover that a Children under 13 has provided me with Personal Information, I will delete such information from our servers immediately.

Changes to this Privacy Statement

Hacklily's privacy statement may change without notice. Changes are effective when they are posted on this website.
